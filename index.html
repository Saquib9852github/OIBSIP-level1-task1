Electricity

An electrical bulb, also known as a light bulb or a lamp, is a device that produces light by heating a filament or a gas inside a glass enclosure. When an electric current flows through the filament, it heats up and emits light in a process called inca ndescence. In the case of gas-filled bulbs, an electric current ionizes the gas, causing it to emit light..

BULB

Electrical bulbs co me in a variety of shapes and sizes and are used for a wide range of applications, from lighting homes and offices to illuminating streets and highways. The brightness of a bulb is measured in lumens, and its power consumption is measured in watts.
